## Overview
These are the general rules I will be using when classifying a hikes difficulty, solitude & beauty
## Difficulty 
*Criteria for a hike to be marked by Trevor as a certain difficulty (may change based on fitness)*
- Goal with the ranking is to keep the average of my ratings around a 5
### 10/10
	Hikes with a full ten means a hospital trip is needed, legitimately. This difficulty will hopefully never be reached as I would die. I envision this as a trail straight up the side of a mountain >30% grade with little to no trail & with enough miles or elevation to warrant very hard
### 9/10
	A hike of this difficulty would be something that would scar a man, keeping him up at night just thinking about how ass this hike was. Probably unable to walk for days after 4-5 days. Envisioned as straight up a mountain with maybe one little switchback, and some class 3-4 scrampling perhaps
### 8/10
	A hike like this is something one may attempt once a summer if crazy. This will have you sore for 2-3 days.
### 7/10
	This is a classic saturday ass kicker, your out there hiking all day and come back tired BUT can do your thing fairly normally the next day and the hike is nothing to go to therapy over
### 6/10
	A more advanced than normal hike, wherein you would want to have some hiking and/or fitness before to not get very tired
### 5/10
	Easy top medium trail condition with some small obsticals (rocks etc) and steep
### 4/10 
	Easy trail with a good incline and spots with steeper incline
### 3/10
	Easy trail with a consistant incline
### 2/10 
	Easy trail with some incline but not a lot
### 1/10 
	A mostly flat stroll, that most will just do on a saturday
## Solitude
This will take into consideration
- Number of people seen on trail
- Trail condition if there is one
- Getting to the hike (trailhead, dirt road etc.)
- My personal opinion on how isolated it feels
## Beauty
This is really just my opinion, but will take into consideration cool finds on the trail as well as the beauty duh.
