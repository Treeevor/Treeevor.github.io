      Overview

      This was our third hike of the [Utah Range High Points](/Posts/Hiking/Utah%20Mountain%20Range%20High Points). Felt very similar to the two before (Craner Peak & Graham Peak) nestled right in the middle. From afar it looks like a smaller & less interesting range than the ones surrounding it, but we found that that is far from true.

      The Hike

      We started from the intersection of the dirt road that approaches the range from the east, and stopped a bit prematurely as to not have any car issues, but you could drive much closer and make this hike a lot easier. From where we started we hiked in a straight line towards the peak, which meant some harder hiking but also allowed us to explore even more. We quickly came upon a cliff side with what looked like a small cave from afar, and after getting close we found the small overhang but more impressive were the rocks. The cliff have veins of white and black rock that formed a intricate maze. Further along there was a rock that had crystals forming out of it. As we progressed we saw massive junipers and sandstone peaking out from the dirt. The straight line though was brutal, going straight up and then right on down loose rock. The final climb to the peak wasn’t easy, from the weird path we had taken it landed us on the steepest part of the face. At the top there is a jar to sign your name, and as expected it hadn’t welcomed many visitors

      Fred’s Take

      Fred, came in to this with a few de-buffs. On the dirt road in he threw up twice and after that we had him chase the car as to not get more sick. However even with that he was full of energy, and had a great time, until he didn’t. Along the way he found many bones from long gone animals, making sure to worry my with the chance of a random parasite. He started to fade after seeing the face of rock we had to climb after going up and down for hours. It was steep but he chugged right on up, but his resolve ran thin near the top. Requiring a small lift up the last obstacle. At the peak he gave the peak 3/10 but after a nice meal, he felt a bit better. On the way down he cheered up and by the time we hit flat ground he forgot about his prior rating. At the car he said 8/10.
